This framework is centered around interaction as being necessary for real language learning. Concepts of this framework include (but are not limited to): noticing, modified output, negotiation for meaning, and corrective feedback.

Corrective feedback is an indication to a learner that an error has occurred, with the aim to revise an interlanguage to a target, modified form.

In instructional practice, interactionist approaches to teaching can be seen in task-based-language-teaching (TBLT), with a focus on producing target forms in output with relevant vocabulary. In a broader context, there are five main categories of corrective feedback that one may encounter in their acquisition efforts.
